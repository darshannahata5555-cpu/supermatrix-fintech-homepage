No. We earn commission from AMCs on Regular Plan schemes; investors do not pay us separately. Direct Plans generally have a lower expense ratio and are available directly through AMCs. We do not facilitate Direct Plans.

Onboarding

Getting Started

How do I start investing with SuperMatrix?

Register on the website or app, complete KYC, link your bank account, and choose a fund or financial goal. Once KYC is verified, you can begin investing.

What is KYC and why do I need it?

KYC (Know Your Customer) is a mandatory identity-verification requirement. It generally uses PAN, address proof, and photo or video verification and usually needs to be completed only once across the mutual fund industry.

What documents do I need?

PAN card, address proof such as Aadhaar or passport, a cancelled cheque or bank statement, and a passport-size photograph. Most onboarding steps can be completed digitally.

Is there a minimum age to invest?

You must be 18 or older to invest independently. A parent or legal guardian can invest on behalf of a minor.

Can NRIs invest through SuperMatrix?

NRIs can generally invest in Indian mutual funds through NRE or NRO bank accounts, subject to FEMA and fund-specific requirements.

Investing & Products

Mutual fund or fixed deposit — what is the difference?

A fixed deposit offers a fixed return set upfront. Mutual fund returns are market-linked and not guaranteed. Equity funds carry more risk but have historically offered greater long-term growth potential than fixed deposits.

SIP or lumpsum — what is the difference?

A SIP invests a fixed amount regularly, while a lumpsum is a one-time investment. SIPs can average market volatility and suit regular savers; lumpsum investing suits investors with capital ready to deploy.

Can I stop or pause my SIP?

Yes. SIPs can generally be paused, modified, or stopped without a penalty, although remaining invested for the long term may help realise the benefit of compounding.

What is ELSS?

ELSS is an equity mutual fund eligible for a deduction under Section 80C, subject to prevailing tax law. It has a three-year lock-in, shorter than PPF or tax-saving fixed deposits.

How is PMS different from a mutual fund?

In PMS, securities are held directly in your demat account and managed for you, rather than pooled into mutual fund units. SEBI mandates a minimum PMS investment of ₹50 lakh.

What is Goal Based Planning?

It maps investments to defined goals such as a home, education, or retirement and creates a savings path and product mix for the target timeframe.

What is an SWP?

A Systematic Withdrawal Plan pays a fixed amount from a mutual fund at regular intervals while the remaining units stay invested.

What is an STP?

A Systematic Transfer Plan transfers a fixed amount regularly from one scheme to another within the same AMC, often moving a lumpsum gradually from a liquid or debt fund into equity.

Understanding costs

Charges, Plans & NAV

What is NAV?

NAV is a scheme’s per-unit market value on a given day, calculated after deducting expenses and liabilities from scheme assets and dividing by outstanding units.

Direct Plan or Regular Plan — what is the difference?

Both use the same underlying portfolio. A Regular Plan is purchased through a distributor and includes distribution commission in its expense ratio. A Direct Plan is purchased from the AMC and generally has a lower expense ratio.

What is Total Expense Ratio (TER)?

TER is the annual cost charged by an AMC for managing and operating a scheme. It is deducted before NAV is published, and SEBI limits the maximum applicable TER.

What is Exit Load?

Exit load is a fee some schemes apply when units are redeemed before a specified holding period. Terms vary by scheme and are disclosed in its offer documents.

Important considerations

Returns, Risk & Taxation

Are mutual fund investments safe?

Mutual funds are regulated by SEBI and assets are held by independent custodians. However, values can rise or fall with markets and returns are not guaranteed.

Can I lose money?

Yes. Equity funds are market-linked, and debt funds also carry risks. Select investments consistent with your risk appetite and time horizon.

What is a riskometer?

The SEBI-mandated riskometer classifies a scheme across six levels from Low to Very High risk, based on its underlying portfolio.

How are returns taxed?

Tax depends on the fund type, holding period, and prevailing law. Rules can change through Union Budgets, so check the latest position or consult a qualified tax professional.

Investor help

Managing Your Account & Support

How can I track investments?

Log in to the SuperMatrix portal or app to view your portfolio, SIP status and returns, and download statements.

What is a folio number?

A folio number is the AMC’s unique account number for recording your purchases, SIPs, and redemptions within that fund house.

Can I add a nominee or joint holder?

Yes, subject to applicable limits and processes. You can nominate beneficiaries with percentage shares and may hold investments jointly under the selected mode of holding.

How do I redeem my investment?

Place a redemption request through your portfolio dashboard. Proceeds are generally credited to the registered bank account within 1–3 business days, depending on fund type.
